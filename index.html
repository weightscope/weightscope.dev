Why it exists

Every fine-tune ships as a multi-gigabyte blob and a vague changelog. When someone uploads Llama-3-70B-Medical-v2, there's no way to see what actually changed vs the base model — you'd have to download both 140 GB blobs and manually compare.

  • Model laundering — rebranded copies uploaded as "new" models, undetected.
  • No quality signal — a LoRA touching 0.1% of weights vs a full fine-tune touching 15% tell you very different things. Currently invisible.
  • Wasted bandwidth — HuggingFace ships petabytes/day of redundant weights because there's no deduplication.

Weightscope is the missing git diff for model weights.
